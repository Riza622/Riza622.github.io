Work

My research interest started with my undergraduate thesis on the gender pay gap in the livestreaming industry. That project made me realize how powerful—and fun—it can be to play with data and explore fascinating economic questions. At Berkeley, I’ve expanded those skills by applying machine learning and statistical methods to a range of real-world problems, from housing displacement and health risks to social trust and digital sentiment. You can find more of my projects below.

Penguin ML Modeling

I worked on a fun little project where I used machine learning to understand penguins better. First, I trained a HistGradientBoostingClassifier to predict species based on physical traits like bill length and flipper size. Then, I used a Neural Network Regressor to estimate body mass. One thing I found interesting was how different combinations of bill and flipper length form distinct zones of predicted weight — the patterns were surprisingly smooth and intuitive once plotted. The visualization helps show these trends clearly and brings the data to life.

Trust in Government Across Age Groups

I used the 2022 General Social Survey (GSS) to examine how trust in the federal government varies across age groups. After constructing key variables including gender, age brackets, and Hispanic status, I applied propensity score weighting based on U.S. Census benchmarks to improve representativeness. The analysis shows that younger adults, especially those aged 25–35, tend to express the highest levels of trust in government, while older groups show a declining trend.

Predicting Music Preferences: A KNN Classifier

I used a K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN) classifier to predict listeners’ music preferences based on acoustic features such as danceability, energy, and tempo. After preprocessing and feature selection, I trained and evaluated the model using an 80/20 train-test split. The classifier achieved an accuracy of approximately 72%, showing strong performance in identifying high- vs. low-rated songs.

Fashion Item Classification with Neural Networks

I trained a neural network to classify fashion items from the Fashion MNIST dataset using TensorFlow and Keras. The model processes 28×28 grayscale images of clothing, shoes, and accessories, and predicts one of 10 predefined categories. The architecture consists of a flatten layer followed by two dense layers (128 units, ReLU) and a final softmax layer. After training for 10 epochs, the model achieved a test accuracy of around 88%, offering a solid baseline for more advanced architectures like CNNs.

Predicting Heart Attack Risk with Logistic Regression

Using a public health dataset, I built a logistic regression model to estimate an individual's risk of heart attack based on clinical indicators such as age, blood pressure, cholesterol, and chest pain type. After data preprocessing and feature engineering, the model was trained and evaluated with metrics including accuracy (~85%), ROC-AUC, and a detailed confusion matrix. The project emphasized explainability and medical interpretability, helping identify key predictors of cardiovascular risk.

Drug Sentiment Classification with NLP

I built a natural language processing (NLP) pipeline to classify sentiment in drug reviews from Drugs.com. The approach includes text preprocessing, TF-IDF vectorization, and training a Logistic Regression model to predict whether a review reflects a positive or negative experience. I evaluated model performance with standard metrics, and used SHAP and coefficient interpretation to identify the most influential terms. This project highlights how machine learning can support public health analysis.

Predicting Titanic Survival with ML

I developed a supervised machine learning pipeline to predict Titanic passenger survival using structured tabular data. Key features include age, sex, passenger class, and fare. The pipeline applies preprocessing techniques, feature engineering, and trains a Gradient Boosting model to classify survival outcomes. I also explored feature importance and decision rules to understand which characteristics most influenced predictions. This project reflects foundational skills in model development, data cleaning, and human-centered ML interpretation.
