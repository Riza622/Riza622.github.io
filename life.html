About Work Life

Life

Things I enjoy outside of research and coding

Reading & Thinking

I enjoy reading broadly across economics, history, and social science, especially work that connects theory with real-world institutions. I’m particularly drawn to books and essays that explain why systems fail, how incentives shape behavior, and how policy interacts with human decision-making.

Walking & Cities

I like walking through cities and paying attention to how neighborhoods change — housing density, public space, transportation, and small details that reflect deeper economic forces. This habit often feeds directly into how I think about urban policy and inequality.

Sports & Staying Active

I enjoy staying active through casual sports and workouts. Physical routines help me stay disciplined and focused, especially during intense academic periods.

Music

I listen to a wide range of music while working or thinking. I’m interested in how personal taste forms and how cultural preferences evolve over time — questions that overlap surprisingly well with data and modeling.

Travel & Perspective

Having studied and lived in multiple countries, I value travel as a way to understand institutions, norms, and everyday life across different contexts. These experiences strongly shape how I approach comparative research questions.
